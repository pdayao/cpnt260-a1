Silken Tofu Recipe

Ingredients

  • Dried Soybeans
  • Coagulant (dissolved in water
    • nigari, food grade gypsum (calcium sulfate), epsom salt
  • Water

How to make silken tofu

  1. Soak dried soybeans overnight.
  2. Remove soybean skin.
  3. In a high speed blender, blend water and soybeans (1:1 ratio).
  4. Using a nutbag, separate pulp and soy milk.
  5. In a large pot, heat soymilk on low to 85 C while stirring. This will prevent the soy milk from burning while it is being heated.
  6. Transfer soy milk into a new container and gently stir in coagulant of choice.
  7. Cover container and let sit for 15 minutes.
  8. Serve warm or chilled.

Notes:

While lemon juice and other citrus additives could work as a coagulant, it will not result in the same smooth texture that silken tofu is known for so using a salt coagulant would work best. It is not recommended to use store bought soy milk to make tofu as they contain preservatives and a much lower protein content than freshly made soy milk (lower protein content = less tofu produced). Soy bean pulp (also known as Okara) can be used in other recipes. They can be added to desserts, blended drinks, and used as an ingredient for protein substitutes!
